Obituary

Jay Mitchell was born on October 28th, 1961 to Gloria Mitchell and Alvin Bailey in the country of Trinidad and Tobago, and at the age of 11 he migrated to the US with his two other siblings, Wendy and Terry Mitchell.


As a young man, Jay was very close with his brother and shared similar hobbies that later helped platform Jay's independent work in music and photography. He also developed an early interest in seeking answers to bible questions and started studying the scriptures with Jehovah’s Witnesses. He was later baptized as one of Jehovah’s Witnesses and dedicated most of his life afterwards as a minister for over 40 years alongside his secular work.


During that time, he returned to his country of birth to meet his lifelong companion, Judith Mitchell whom he married in March 1989. The family quickly grew to four after fathering two children, Jeremy and Jermaine Mitchell.


As a child and adult, Jay has always been was both reserved and confident, serious with a smile, a straight-forward and honest man who said exactly how he felt. He was well known by friends and family for his gentle spirit and willingness to help anyone in need. He had a natural talent for cooking and was often praised for his pepper sauce, fried fish, stew chicken, and truly nothing made him happier than seeing his friends satisfied.


Jay has touched our hearts with his unique character and caring spirit. He will truly be missed.


Jay leaves behind: His wife Judith Mitchell, sons Jeremy and Jermaine Mitchell. His sister Wendy Mitchell. His nieces Nicole and Shanea Mitchell, and her son Elijah Mitchell. His uncles Trevor and Junior Mitchell. Rudy Ann Mitchell, his cousins, in-laws and friends all of whom loved him dearly.


It is our belief that we look forward to seeing our dear loved one again in the future, in a paradise; on earth.

The Mitchell family would like to express gratitude to everyone for their kind words of encouragement, sincere sympathy, earnest prayers and outpouring of love at this difficult time. We recognize that some of you even risked your own lives to ensure we were physically sustained and for this we are ever so grateful.
